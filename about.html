About me

Who am I

Hi! My name is Maggie and I work as a nurse and I am a part-time traveler and blogger. As a little girl I used to dream about going differnt places all over the world, to see iconic places and experience other cultures. Traveling is expensive, you either need to save up money in advance or you could "simply" make a living of your travels. I do love to travel and I wanted to do more of that, but I love my job, so I didn't want to stop that. So I began to research on how I could combine both, and now I take 4-6 trips a year and I get to check of destinations on my bucket list. How cool is that?

I know I just said that traveling is expensive, but it doesn’t have to be. And with that being said, it is all about prioritizing. Not all trips I go on are equally long or have an equal level of luxury. And by luxury I mean fancy restaurants, because I love a great meal, high-end hotels and expensive activities. I always set goals and expectations in advance, so I can begin to plan my trip. Sometimes I want some luxury on my trips with fancy food and fancy hotel and other times I’m good with homemade food and staying at low-key motels or even outdoors!

101 on How to travel in Maggie Style

First of all plane-tickets can be really cheap if you have a flexible schedule or order them well ahead of the trip. First of all a google-search goes a long way. By googling you wanted travel you will get a lot of different offers from travel agencies, airlines, bus and train-companies, which makes it easy to compare. There are also sites that you can compare travels routes by price and estimated time. Many airlines have a «low-price»-calendars where you can get very cheap-tickets. Bus and train can have nice prices, you also might be able to travel further for less, so always check out that opportunity!

Where to stay

Research your stay in advance. There are many options as hotels, motels, air bnb’s, bnb’s. Maybe you can use an outdoor tent or maybe you know someone who lives there that you can visit and have a couch you can sleep on.

What to eat

Great food can be expensive and great food can be really cheap. Do some research in advance. Book tables at the restaurants you know you want to eat at, so you don’t risk that it’s fully booked. Also make sure that you can eat spontaneously, because you can find little gems in back-alleys. If you have the opportunity, it is always nice to make some food of your own because you will get tired of eating out - especially if you’re traveling over time.
